Effective date: 30 May 2022

This document describes how Volvo Cars (as defined below) processes your personal data when you as a retailer employee participate in various types of training and certification assessments,  (hereinafter “Retailer training”).

You can find below:

  1. Who is responsible for the processing of your personal data
  2. What personal data we process and why
  3. How long we keep your data
  4. Who we share your personal data with
  5. What rights you have in relation to the data processing we perform
  6. How to contact us

  1. Who we are

The entity responsible for the processing of personal data in relation to the Volvo Cars Retailer Trainings  is Volvo Car Corporation, having its registered office at 405 31, Göteborg, Sweden, company registration number 556074-3089 hereinafter referred to as “Volvo Cars”, “we”, or “us”.

  1. What personal data we collect and why

Below you will find various training applications, systems and portals within Volvo Cars and how your data is processed. In all of the below cases, we will use your information to assess how to improve the services provided on the applications, systems and portals, as well as visualize your data in an analytics tool to simplify retrieving reports and statistics data. We will do this because it is our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f) to simplify the reporting and vizualisation process, to better understand and meet your training needs. The retention will follow each retention time of the applications, systems and platform, please find more information in section 3.

  1. Volvo Cars Performance Academy (VCPA)

The personal data that we will process is your contact information such as name, e-mail address, training information such as certification history, and professional information such as dealer name, job role as well as your login name, preferred language and articles/courses viewed, search words used and downloaded content. Technical information about your sessions (login, logout, time stamp) will also be collected. For some markets also gender (Spain and China), mobile number and birth date (only China).    

The purpose of our processing is to manage (schedule, book and follow up) training courses; and to communicate updates related to your training, competence performance, and certification. Furthermore, it will also be used for providing you access to other relevant supporting applications for training (such as the Technical Reference Information Portal or TRIP), technical documentations and for statistical purposes.

The legal basis for our process as described above is our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f) to fullfil the contract that we have with your employer in regards to providing training courses, relevant product information and to further develop the competences for the people that work together with us.  

  1. Virtual Venue

We use your contact information such as name and e-mail address, information about your training and learning scorecard as well as data related to your visit to the Virtual Venue such as interactions, views, logins, time spent as well as interactions and interaction channels. We use this information because it is our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f) to secure that you can access all information, trainings, video presentations and all other functionalities properly within the venue and so that we can monitor and follow up on trainings.

  1. Commercial Product Information Platform (CPIP)

The personal data that we will process is your contact information such as name and e-mail address, as well as CDSID. We will also use information about the articles you have viewed, the search words you have used as  well as data on downloaded articles. The purpose of our processing is to manage training courses; and to communicate updates related to your training.

The legal basis for our process as described above is our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f) to fulfil the contract that we have with your employer in regards to providing training courses, relevant product information and to further develop the competences for the people that work together with us.  

  1. VISTA Competition

We use the personal data that you provide to us for administration and to facilitate your participation in the VISTA Competition. We will use your name, contact details, gender, employment information and your results during the competition. If needed – additional personal information for supporting the Visa-application process. This we do based on our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f).

If applicable, we will also collect and use the photos and videos that you have provided as part of the VISTA Competition in order to administer the competition. This is done with both your  consent (GDPR Art. 6.1.a) and the consent of other individuals appearing in the photos or videos that you provide. You and your colleagues have the right to withdraw this consent at any time. Please use the form mentioned below should you wish to do so.

  1. Volvo Virtual Maintenance Trainer (VVMT)

We use your contact details such as name and email address, as well as your learning scorecard information including score, pass/fail and duration of the training sessions because it is our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6.1.f) to monitor participation and completion of the trainings.

  1. How long we keep your data

Please find below how long we keep your data per system and/or training opportunity:

  1. Volvo Cars Performance Academy (VCPA)

We will retain your personal data until the training course is deleted from our learning catalogue or at the latest up to five years after your account has been inactivated. Thereafter, your personal data will be anonymized and the information will be used for statistical purposes.

  1. Virtual Venue

We will retain your personal data for five years after your account has been inactivated. Thereafter, your personal data will be deleted.

  1. Commercial Product Information Platform (CPIP)

We will retain your personal data until the training course is deleted from our learning catalogue or at the latest up to five years after your account has been inactivated. Thereafter, your personal data will be anonymized and the information will be used for statistical purposes.

  1. VISTA Competition

We will keep the competition results and add them to your file once the competition has ended. We will retain these up to five years following termination of your employment.

If applicable, and as an exception to the above, all data collected for supporting the VISA application process and travel arrangements will be deleted within 3 months from the end of the competition.

If you have provided photos, videos or other content during the competition – we will delete this data either one year from the end of the competition or directly in the case where you withdraw your consent.

  1. Volvo Virtual Maintenance Trainer (VVMT)

We will retain your personal data for five years after your account has been inactivated, after which it will be deleted.

  1. Who we share your personal data with

We will share your personal data with the following categories of third parties, on a need-to-know basis:

  • Our processors supporting our activity in general, such as providers of IT solutions;
  • Our processors providing training and documentation material as well as event organizers, which are limited by contract in their ability to use your personal data for any purpose other than to provide services for us in compliance with each data processing agreement in place;
  • Watershed – the USA – Analytics. Personal data will be transferred to the USA, on the basis of the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the EU Commission, in accordance with Art. 46(2)(d) GDPR. The general text of the standard contractual clauses used can be found in various languages on the homepage of the EU Commission at EUR-Lex - 32021D0914 - EN - EUR-Lex (europa.eu); a copy of the agreement may be provided upon request.

  1. Your rights in relation to the data processing we perform

You have specific legal rights granted by the General Data Protection Regulation relating to the personal data we process about you. You can withdraw your consent or object to our processing of your data, access the data we hold about you, ask for rectification or restriction of your data, request to have your data ported to another entity, request that we delete your data, and finally you can file a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority.

Details about your rights – what they mean, when and how you can exercise them – can be found in our Customer Privacy Policy.
